Is the gender pay gap affected by factors of one's education? I am using data from the IPUMS American Community Survey which supplies me with information on wages and the highest level of education one has completed. From this research, my hypothesis is that women will still earn less than men even if they have a higher level of education. This hypothesis is based on well researched patterns in economics and sociology that women are more likely to end up in lower paying occupations and face discrimination in hiring and promotions. This usually results in lower returns to the same credentials as men. 
In this data analysis, gender is the explanatory variable and education level are added to see if they help to explain or change the size of the gender pay gap. My outcome variable is the wage or income measured in dollars. If the regression shows that the pay gap remains after controlling the education variables, then that would support my hypothesis. If the gap disappears when these factors are added, then it would disprove my hypothesis.
